## New Discoveries

### 1. Whisper Speaker Identification (WSI) - NEW MODEL!
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/jakariaemon/WSI
- **Paper:** arXiv 2503.10446 (March 2025)
- **Innovation:** Adapts Whisper encoder for speaker identification
- **Key Features:**
  - Multilingual speaker identification
  - Uses projection head with hybrid loss (Online Triplet + Multi-View Self-Supervised)
  - Language-agnostic speaker embeddings
  - State-of-the-art EER and F1 scores on multilingual datasets
- **Status:** Coming Soon (not yet usable)

### 2. DiCoW v2 - Diarization-Conditioned Whisper
- **Released:** March 27, 2025
- **GitHub Discussion:** https://github.com/openai/whisper/discussions/264
- **Key Improvement:** Takes diarization as input, transcribes multi-talker speech even when speakers speak DIFFERENT LANGUAGES
- **Use Case:** Meetings with multilingual participants
- **Papers Available:** Multiple papers from BUT/JHU CHiME-8 challenge

### 3. "Name of the..." - Self-Hosted Speaker ID App
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/JonGerhardson/name-of-the
- **Updated:** May 5, 2025 (Docker support, GPU support, speaker ID memory)
- **Stack:**
  - faster-whisper for transcription
  - pyannote.audio for diarization
  - speechbrain/spkrec-ecapa-voxceleb for speaker embeddings
  - FAISS for similarity search
- **Features:**
  - Web UI for uploading files
  - Speaker enrollment/management
  - Remembers speaker IDs across transcripts
  - Whisper prompting support

### 4. Target Speaker ASR with Whisper (ICASSP 2025)
- **Paper:** Submitted to ICASSP 2025 (BUT)
- **Status:** Code not yet released
- **Focus:** Target speaker extraction for ASR

### 5. E-SHARC: End-to-End Supervised Hierarchical Graph Clustering
- **Published:** IEEE/ACM TASLP, 2025 (IISC)
- **Approach:** Graph clustering for speaker diarization
